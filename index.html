Getting there & around

Waiheke Island is approximately 45 minutes by ferry from the Auckland CBD. Fullers ferries usually leave the downtown Ferry Terminal once an hour, and the Devonport ferry terminal once every two hours within a limited time frame. If you are flying into Auckland on Friday evening, make sure you check the time of the last ferry against your expected arrival time - usually the last sailing is 11:45pm. Show your wedding invitation when you purchase your ticket to receive discounted tickets.

Sealink Ferries have both car and passenger ferries that depart from Half Moon Bay and Wynyard Quarter every 1-2 hours. If you want to get the car ferry, email Rachael as we can get you discounted tickets. Note: You need to check-in 30 minutes before sailing.

Or, if you're feeling really fancy, you could charter a helicopter.

Once you're on the island, there are a number of ways of getting around including taxis, buses and rental cars.

Waiheke Accommodation

Waiheke has a huge range of accommodation, including beachfront apartments, luxury lodges, boutique hotels and kiwi baches (holiday houses). If you are travelling as a group, you may want to book an apartment or a larger home. If you are travelling as a couple, there are smaller homes available to rent, or you can choose from a range of lodges/B&Bs. We recommend www.bookabach.co.nz and www.waihekeholidayhomes.co.nz.

The wedding will be held in Surfdale, so we recommend staying nearby, on the western side of the island. Oneroa is probably the busiest area, with plenty of shops and cafes. It's also close to the Matiatia Bay ferry terminal. Onetangi and Palm Beach are a bit quieter, and Ostend is central if you don't mind not being near a beach.

If you want to watch the Rugby World Cup final (5:00 NZDT on the 1st of November), make sure you book accommdation with SkyTV.

Waiheke Activities

Waiheke is most famous for wine tasting! If you love wine, we recommend you do a winery tour the day after or before the wedding. We always do tours with Max Walker, a Waiheke local, but there are a number of options available. Fullers has a Vineyard Hopper bus, which you can buy a pass for in conjunction with your ferry ticket, and the Waiheke Island Tripadvisor page lists a number of other wine tour companies.

Waiheke Island also has a number of great restaurants. Our favourites are Casita Miro and Stonyridge for a casual lunch/dinner, and Te Whau (make sure you ask for a table on the balcony) or Cable Bay for something a bit more upmarket.

We are planning on having a casual rehearsal dinner the night before the wedding at Casita Miro. If you like the look of their menu, we would love to see you there. Click here for their reservations page.

Auckland

Getting around: If you are planning on staying in Auckland/New Zealand for longer than the weekend, we recommend you hire a car. All the major rental car companies operate out of Auckland airport. If you're just visiting for the weekend, or spending all your time on Waiheke, you can probably get away without a car. If you're not hiring a car, we recommend booking a shuttle from the airport to your accommodation/the CBD in advance. Taxis are available, but are considerably more expensive for just one or two people, and the advertised "Airbus Express" can take up to an hour to get from the airport to the CBD. Bus and train services operate between the CBD and surrounding suburbs, see the AT (Auckland Transport) website for details.

Where to stay: If you are just in New Zealand for the weekend, we recommend spending the whole time on Waiheke Island. However, if you want to spend a night or two in Auckland itself, we recommend staying in the CBD for easy access to transport.
